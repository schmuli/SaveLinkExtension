The Daily WTF: The forums are weighing the advantages of Agile vs. Waterfall. +Remy Porter 's stance: Agile is not always (or even often) the right answer, but Waterfall almost always is the wrong way to deliver software. I've been on a number of very successful Agile projects that over-delivered and ended under budget. Agile provides a lot of opportunity to work closely with the customer and actually figure out what they want, instead of what they specified. But if you're not careful about how you manage the team, it's very easy to see it explode in a million little recriminations and failures.  There's no one right way to deliver software, but there are very many wrong ones.
